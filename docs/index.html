GooD Autobackuper

Automated Backup System for Google Drive

Relevance

Information infrastructure is based on data storage systems of various sizes, scales, and methods of data transmission and processing.
Cloud storage services such as Google Drive, Amazon Cloud Drive, Dropbox, Microsoft OneDrive, Apple iCloud, and others are widely used both by enterprises and individuals for data storage.

Among them, Google Drive is the most popular, with over 2 billion active users as of 2025 [1]. Dropbox ranks second, used by approximately 66.20% of cloud service users (as of 2020) [2].

Special attention is paid to information protection—backup is the process of creating a copy of data to enable recovery in the event of corruption or loss [3].

Compared to backing up data to personal storage devices, 63% of users who perform backups use cloud systems as their primary backup method [4].

According to a Western Digital survey, only 87% of respondents perform backups (either automatically or manually). Furthermore, 63% of respondents have already experienced data loss due to device failure, accidental deletion, or cyberattacks [5]. Additionally, 63% of users said they would back up their data more often if the process were automated.
